You Just Got Phished!

This was a simulated phishing exercise. Your real credentials are safe — but here's what you missed.

What happened? You just submitted your login credentials to a fake website designed to look like the University of Karachi Student Portal. In a real attack, a hacker would now have your username and password. This exercise is meant to help you spot phishing attempts before it's too late.

🔍 Red Flags You Missed

🌐

Fake Domain Name

The real KU portal uses the official government domain. This site used a lookalike domain.

❌ ku-studentportal.edu.pk
✅ uok.edu.pk
🔒

SSL ≠ Safety

The padlock icon means the connection is encrypted — but it does not mean the site is legitimate. Attackers can get free SSL certificates too.

❌ Padlock ≠ Trusted Site
📧

Suspicious Link Source

Did you receive this link via WhatsApp, SMS, or email from an unknown sender? Legitimate portals are bookmarked or typed manually.

❌ Link from unknown source
⚡

Urgency Tactics

Phishing pages often create panic — "Your account will be suspended!" or "Login now to view results." This pressures you to act without thinking.

❌ Never act under pressure
🎨

Copy-Paste Design

Attackers copy the look and feel of real sites exactly. Visual similarity does NOT confirm authenticity — always check the URL first.

❌ Looks real ≠ Is real
🏛️

No Official Verification

Real university portals are announced through official channels: notice boards, .edu.pk emails, and official social media pages.

✅ Always verify via official sources

🛡️ How to Stay Safe

1
Always check the URL bar before entering credentials. Look for the exact official domain (e.g., uok.edu.pk), not lookalikes.
2
Never click links in SMS or WhatsApp messages that ask for your login. Go directly to the site by typing the address.
3
Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) wherever possible. Even if your password is stolen, 2FA adds an extra layer of security.
4
Use a password manager. It will only auto-fill credentials on the correct domain — and refuse to fill on fake sites.
5
When in doubt, call the IT helpdesk directly to verify if a login page or link is legitimate.
6
Report suspicious links to your university's IT department immediately so others can be warned.

Stay Alert, Stay Safe 🔐
